# QuestionWork Growth Gap Map

Date: 2026-03-12
Scope: ближайшие 3 месяца
Goal: определить, чего не хватает проекту, чтобы увеличить спрос со стороны заказчиков, предложение со стороны фрилансеров, конверсию воронки и повторные сделки.

## Current Positioning
- Product already has strong thematic differentiation: RPG/guild fantasy is visible on the landing page, quest board, and public profiles.
- Engineering baseline is materially stronger after the audit fixes.
- Public surfaces exist for demand and supply discovery: landing page, quest board, user directory, public user profiles.
- Core gap: the product feels branded and feature-rich, but not yet instrumented or structured as a growth system.

## Main Diagnosis
QuestionWork currently looks closer to a stylized marketplace prototype with strong atmosphere than to a marketplace with a deliberate liquidity engine.

The missing layer is not mostly UI. The missing layer is the operating system for growth:
- acquisition loops
- trust and proof systems for buyers
- structured supply quality signals
- funnel instrumentation
- repeat-transaction loops
- CRM and lifecycle messaging

## 3-Month Gap Map

### 1. Demand Generation Gaps
Missing:
- clear client-specific landing flow and value proposition
- client-focused pages by use case, stack, and urgency
- inbound capture funnels for leads before registration
- SEO program around service categories and specialist types
- lightweight outbound/CRM workflow for demand capture
- proof assets for clients: case studies, outcomes, SLA-style promises, trust copy

Consequence:
- the product can look interesting, but not yet obviously low-risk to a buyer with budget.

### 2. Supply Quality Gaps
Missing:
- stronger freelancer profile proof: portfolio, verified outcomes, response time, completion rate, average budget, specialization score
- clearer ranking logic for the talent marketplace
- curated top talent / featured guilds / verified experts layer
- freelancer onboarding that turns a new account into a credible profile fast
- supply quality segmentation by stack, seniority, availability, timezone, price band

Consequence:
- demand that does arrive may not convert because supply is not legible enough.

### 3. Funnel Conversion Gaps
Missing:
- end-to-end funnel analytics for registration -> quest creation -> application -> hire -> completion
- event taxonomy and dashboards for product decisions
- client-side onboarding assistant for posting the first quest
- templates and guided quest creation for common job types
- trust-reduction mechanisms during hiring: shortlist compare, recommendation hints, response SLAs, interview scheduling
- better empty states and recovery flows when users see low liquidity

Consequence:
- even with traffic, conversion will leak because the marketplace asks users to infer too much.

### 4. Retention and Repeat Gaps
Missing:
- repeat-hire workflows for clients
- saved talent, saved filters, saved searches, and alerts
- lifecycle messaging: incomplete profile nudges, first quest reminders, application follow-ups, completion reactivation
- buyer re-engagement after successful quest completion
- freelancer return loops: streaks tied to work activity, not just ambient RPG progression
- post-transaction CRM and satisfaction capture

Consequence:
- successful transactions do not yet obviously compound into future marketplace activity.

### 5. Operating Gaps
Missing:
- a quarterly metric model with owner-level KPIs
- segmentation of users by side, lifecycle stage, and quality tier
- experiment cadence and A/B test discipline
- acquisition attribution and source tracking
- CRM tool choice and notification strategy
- explicit marketplace liquidity playbook for low-supply / low-demand categories

Consequence:
- the team can ship features without knowing which lever actually changes growth.

## Recommended Strategic Focus
Recommendation: do not try to scale all four directions equally.

Primary focus for the next 3 months:
1. Increase client trust and quest posting conversion.
2. Make freelancer supply legible and comparable.
3. Build lifecycle loops for repeat hiring and return visits.
4. Add instrumentation so future prioritization is evidence-based.

## Priority Tiers

### Tier 1: Must ship this quarter
- Client-first acquisition surfaces
- Guided quest posting and templates
- Trust layer for buyers
- Stronger freelancer proof profiles
- Core funnel analytics
- Repeat-hire and reactivation basics

### Tier 2: Should ship if capacity remains
- Saved searches / alerts
- Featured experts / featured guilds
- segmented landing pages
- CRM automations and digest messaging

### Tier 3: Can wait
- deep social/guild loops
- advanced recommendations
- full realtime demand-supply matching
- heavy gamification expansion not tied to transaction outcomes

## Risks If Not Addressed
- strong design but weak buyer confidence
- traffic without conversion insight
- supply that looks flavorful but not trustworthy enough for paid work
- one-off deals without compounding retention

## Draft North Star For Planning
Over the next 90 days, QuestionWork should evolve from a themed freelance marketplace into a trust-oriented, measurable, repeatable hiring system where a client can quickly understand why to post, whom to hire, and why to come back.
